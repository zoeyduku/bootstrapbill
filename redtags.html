What are the benefits of installing a Humidifier?

Most of the tissue in our bodies is composed of water. When we lose water our skin dries up and the symptoms of dehydration begin to show including chronic joint and muscle pain, raspy throat, sore eyes and lack of mental concentration. The same goes for musicians and their instruments; if the humidity isn't properly maintained then their instruments will begin to warp and crack. The most common way to combat dehydration is to drink a lot of water. The other, less common method is to maintain the relative humidity in your home with a Furnace Humidifier. Ask our CoolHeat Comfort Technicians how to optimize the relative humidity in your home.

What does AFUE stand for?

There are special names for the efficiency ratings of various types of equipment. Furnaces are rated according to their Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency, or AFUE. The higher the AFUE rating, the more efficient the unit.

What is a Red Tag?

Everytime a Certified Gas Technician is servicing or hooking up gas to your Home they inspect your Heating System to ensure the equipment is operating safely. If it doesn't meet the Natural Gas and Propane Installation Code or the Manufacturer’s Certified Instructions then as a safety precaution the Certified Gas Technician is required by law to issue the TSSA Warning("Red") Tag.

Types of Red Tags

Type A

System is an Immediate Hazard

An "A" Tag is required when the Heating System posses an immediate threat to everyone's safety. The Gas Supply for the System, or sometimes building, is shut off. Common examples include a cracked heat exchanger that cannot be repaired and improper ignition.

Type B

Appliance or Work Does NOT Constitute an Immediate Hazard

"B" Tags are issued when the safety threat is minimal to the building's occupants. The Homeowner is given a limited time frame for repairs or replacement. If they don't fix the problem within the given time the natural gas supply will be shut off. Common examples include improper clearance to combustibles and insufficient pipe supports.

How do I fix my Red Tagged System?

The fixes and the "Work Completed By" form must be completed and submitted to Enbridge at least 5 business days before the Red Tag's Expiry Date. Failing to include all the necessary information or returning the form late can result in a service visit from Enbridge. If they have to visit your home to verify the repairs they'll bill you a $70 service charge plus tax on your Enbridge account. So make sure you're on top of things and get your "B" Tagged System fixed ASAP.

For "A" Tags

System Replacement

To ensure your safety, every Certified Gas Technician is legally required to shut off the gas if your Heating System received an “A” Tag. To get the Hot Air flowing again and fix that Red Tag you'll most likely need to replace your Heating System before a Gas Tech deems it safe enough to remove the Warning Tag.

For "B" Tags

System Repairs or Replacement

Receiving a Tag means it's your responsibility to fix your Heating System. You have 35 days to have your "B" Tagged Heating System repaired or replaced before the Tag expires and your Gas is shut off. Only a Certifed Gas Technician has the power to deem your Heating System safe and remove the Warning Tag.
